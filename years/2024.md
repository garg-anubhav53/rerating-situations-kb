# 2024 — Re-Rating Situations

**Thesis of the year:** 2024 was the year the AI trade broke out of the chips and into the **power,
grid, and physical infrastructure that feeds them** — the single biggest re-rating cluster was the
AI-electricity complex (VST, GEV, VRT, TLN, POWL, SMR, NNE), repriced on the realization that
data-center load growth had ended two decades of flat US power demand. Alongside it ran three other
engines: **MicroStrategy's leveraged bitcoin-treasury flywheel** (MSTR) that defined crypto-equity and
spawned copycats (SMLR, BTCS); a cluster of **left-for-dead small-caps that turned profitable and
re-rated violently** (ROOT, SEZL, AppLovin's AXON engine at large-cap scale); and the year's most
*surprising* multibaggers, which hid in **micro-caps** — AST SpaceMobile's direct-to-cell satellites,
TSS Inc's data-center rack-integration niche, and the Nov–Dec **quantum retail mania** (QUBT) touched
off by Google's Willow chip. Two macro/political re-ratings round it out: the **Milei disinflation
trade** that ~4x'd Argentine banks (GGAL/BMA/BBAR/SUPV) and YPF, and the **Trump-election** repricing
of private-prison capacity (GEO). India PSU/railway/defense names (RVNL, COCHINSHIP, IFCI) doubled in a
domestic retail frenzy.

**Pattern that recurs:** the fastest, largest re-ratings came where a *narrative regime change* (AI needs
power; bitcoin-on-the-balance-sheet; a left-for-dead unit reaches profitability) met a *small float or
small base*. The megacaps that doubled (NVDA, APP) are notable precisely because doubling at $15B–$1T is
hard; the 7–40x names all started sub-$500M.
