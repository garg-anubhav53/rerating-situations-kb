# 2023 — Re-Rating Situations

**Thesis of the year:** 2023 had **two engines that lifted almost every multi-bagger**, plus a set of
regional manias. Engine one was the **birth of the AI trade** — NVIDIA (+239%) was the epicenter, and its
gravity pulled up the "Magnificent Seven" reflation (META's "Year of Efficiency" recovery, AMD, AVGO) and
the AI-hardware supply chain (SMCI more than tripled; Taiwan's Wiwynn/Alchip; Japan's Advantest). Engine
two was **bitcoin's +156% recovery**, which leveraged every crypto miner off a tiny base into 3–10x
(MARA +688%, RIOT +332%, CLSK +221%, CIFR, BTBT, WULF, BITF, MIGI) and re-rated the BTC proxies (MSTR,
COIN). Overlaid on those were three regional/thematic re-rating clusters: **Korea's retail-driven
secondary-battery mania** (Ecopro +571% — the *world's single best-performing stock of 2023* — plus Kum
Yang, Cosmo AM&T, EcoPro BM); **India's PSU / railway / defense capex re-rating** (RVNL, IRFC, Suzlon,
Mazagon Dock, GRSE, Titagarh, Jupiter Wagons); and a wave of **biotech clinical-catalyst re-ratings**
(Madrigal's NASH data, Akero, Praxis, Ambrx, and the early GLP-1 halo on Viking). Carvana ran ~10x on a
debt-restructuring turnaround from the brink of bankruptcy; VinFast spiked ~9x intra-year on an absurd
de-SPAC low float before round-tripping; and the year's most *off-radar* winners hid in unfollowed
nano-caps — Alarum (residential-proxy data), Mawson (BTC hosting), Direct Digital (ad-tech). The Milei
election (Nov 2023) lit the Argentine-financials trade (GGAL, YPF, PAM) into year-end.

**Pattern that recurs:** the megacap doublers (NVDA, META, AMD) are notable because doubling at
$100B–$1T is *hard* and signals a genuine regime change (AI capex is real). But the fastest, largest
multiples came where **leverage to a moving underlying** (BTC price → miners) or **a small float caught in
a retail mania** (Korea batteries; VinFast) or **a policy/capex re-rating** (India PSU) met a **small
base**. The single most instructive names are the ones that ran with *no press* — the microcap that 8–10x'd
on a screen, not a headline.

## Deep write-ups

### CVNA — Carvana (NYSE) · ~$3.80 → ~$45+ · ~10–11x · start mkt-cap ~$600M
**What the market misunderstood:** By late 2022, the market had priced CVNA as a near-certain bankruptcy: a used-car e-tailer that had over-expanded (ADESA acquisition, physical reconditioning centers) into a rate-shock collapse, sitting under ~$6.5B of debt with negative unit economics and burning cash. Short interest hit ~58% of float (~54M shares). The consensus error was treating a *liquidity/duration* problem as a *solvency/business-model* problem. The core marketplace still sold cars profitably per-unit; what was broken was the balance sheet and the cost structure bolted on during the ZIRP land-grab — both fixable without equity wipeout, especially with a large founder/Garcia family stake motivated to avoid Chapter 11.

**What actually changed — business vs. re-rating:**
- *Business change (fundamentals):* Aggressive cost cuts (SG&A slashed, ADESA integration, headcount reductions) plus a mix shift toward higher-margin loan sales and reconditioning efficiency drove gross-profit-per-unit to records — Q2 2023 non-GAAP GPU of ~$7,030 (GAAP ~$6,520), up ~36% over the prior best, aided by ~$900/unit of non-recurring benefits. The company flipped to positive adjusted EBITDA (~$155M in Q2, margin ~5.2%), the pivotal proof-of-life. The July 2023 debt exchange cut total debt by ~$1.2–1.3B and lowered required *cash* interest by ~$430M+/year for two years (via PIK), removing the bankruptcy clock.
- *Re-rating change (narrative/multiple):* Odds of survival went from "priced for zero" to "going concern." That repricing, layered on a ~$600M float that was ~half short, produced a violent short squeeze — the bulk of the ~10x move is multiple/narrative re-rating, not proportional fundamental gain.

**Mechanism & timeline:** (1) Jan 2023 — stock troughs near $4, max pessimism/max short interest. (2) Q1 report + cost-cut evidence begins the doubt-removal. (3) May–July — a creditor group (Apollo, ~90%+ of unsecured notes) negotiates the exchange; **July 19** the deal is announced alongside a strong Q2 pre-release and shares jump ~40% in a day. (4) Q2 record GPU + positive EBITDA validates the turn; squeeze accelerates through summer. (5) Restructuring completes Sept 1; stock grinds to the high-$50s intraday and closes 2023 up ~10x.

**Transferable lesson:** Separate *balance-sheet distress* from *business-model failure*. The asymmetric long is a left-for-dead name where (a) the going business still throws off positive unit economics, (b) the actual problem is refinanceable/restructurable rather than terminal, (c) insiders are aligned to avoid wipeout, and (d) a crowded short (>40–50% of a tiny float) supplies convex fuel. When "priced for bankruptcy" meets a credible survival catalyst, the first derivative that matters is *survival odds*, not earnings — and the re-rating dwarfs the fundamental delta.

### ALAR — Alarum Technologies (NASDAQ) · ~10x+ in 2023 · start mkt-cap ~$15M
**What the market misunderstood:** ALAR entered 2023 as the discarded husk of Safe-T Group — a perennially money-losing Israeli cybersecurity/consumer-privacy micro-cap that had reverse-split and diluted its way to a ~$15M market cap with zero US analyst coverage. Buried inside the shell was NetNut: an enterprise residential-proxy IP network that lets customers (data aggregators, price/SEO scrapers, AI/e-commerce firms) route web-scraping traffic through a hybrid pool of real residential and static ISP IPs to collect public web data at scale and bypass anti-bot blocking. The market was still pricing a dying cyber company; almost no one had modeled the proxy business hiding underneath it.

**What actually changed — business vs. re-rating:** *Business change* was real and fast. NetNut data-collection revenue grew from ~$8.5M (2022) to ~$21.3M (2023), up ~150% YoY, dragging total continuing revenue to ~$26.5M (+~43%). Gross margin hit a record ~77%, and the company flipped from cash-burning to profitable — ~$1.1M net profit and ~$1.9M adjusted EBITDA in Q3, then a record ~$1.7M net profit / ~$2.2M adjusted EBITDA in Q4 (vs. a ~$2.0M adj. EBITDA loss in Q4'22). Management also formalized the pivot: in July 2023 it scaled the legacy consumer/privacy business down "to a minimum," concentrating the whole company on NetNut. *Re-rating change* was discovery — an unfollowed nano-cap with an accelerating, high-margin, now-profitable SaaS-like data business getting found by momentum and small-cap investors, compressing years of skepticism into a few quarters.

**Mechanism & timeline:** Jan 2023 — Safe-T rebrands to Alarum, signaling the NetNut-first identity. Q1–Q2 — each quarterly 6-K prints a bigger NetNut number and rising margin; by 9M'23 revenue (~$19.4M) already exceeded all of FY2022. July 2023 — explicit decision to wind down the consumer segment. Q3'23 — first clean net profit + record adj. EBITDA, the "it's actually profitable now" catalyst. Into early 2024 the trailing move was cited at ~+1,250%, i.e. a 10x+ re-rating driven far more by discovered earnings power than by multiple hope.

**Transferable lesson:** The highest-payoff pattern in this KB is the *hidden operating asset inside a de-rated shell*. Screen for nano-caps where a legacy narrative (here: failing cybersecurity) masks a single segment growing triple digits at high gross margin and approaching cash breakeven — read the segment footnotes, not the headline. The signal to act *before* discovery: a name change / strategic "focus" language plus two consecutive quarters of accelerating segment revenue and an EBITDA inflection, while sell-side coverage is still zero. When the real business crosses into GAAP profit with no analysts watching, the re-rating from "unfollowed" to "momentum name" can be 10x on the business turn alone.

### 086520.KS — Ecopro Co (KOSDAQ, Korea) · ~+571% · ~6.7x · world's #1 stock of 2023 · start mkt-cap ~$2.5B
**What the market misunderstood / the dislocation:** Ecopro Co is a *holding company* whose principal asset is a ~40%+ stake in listed cathode maker EcoPro BM (247540), plus EcoPro HN and unlisted Ecopro Materials. Rationally a holdco trades at a *discount* to its look-through NAV (double taxation, no operating control premium). Through 2023 Ecopro Co instead re-rated to a market cap that at times *exceeded* the fair value of its stake in BM plus other holdings — an inverted, negative-conglomerate-discount premium that is a textbook flow-driven anomaly. This was not a mispriced-fundamentals story; it was a small-float, retail-dominated instrument where price detached from any NAV or DCF anchor. Analysts near the peak had price targets a fraction of the market price and were widely ignored.

**What actually changed — business vs. re-rating:** *Business:* real but incremental — EV-cathode capacity expansion, a high-nickel order pipeline tied to the US IRA/anti-China tailwind, and an LFP roadmap. Group earnings grew, but nothing remotely proportional to a ~6.7x move; H2 2023 profitability actually softened with cathode/lithium prices. *Re-rating:* overwhelmingly flows. Korean retail piled into the entire "secondary-battery" complex ("Korea's GameStop"), egged on by YouTube influencers ("Battery Aju-ma" following); Ecopro became the most-crowded retail name. A vicious short squeeze compounded it — Ecopro/EcoPro BM were among the *most-shorted* KOSDAQ stocks, and the Nov 5 2023 FSC short-selling ban (through mid-2024) forced covering, spiking both names to +30% limit-up. MSCI Korea inclusion (Aug 2023) added index-driven buying. Treat this as a re-rating/flows event, not a fundamental one.

**Mechanism & timeline:** Pre-split, ~110,000 won at Jan open; parabolic through H1, peaking near ~1.5M won in mid/late July (roughly +900% intra-year at the top). A sharp ~40–50% give-back into autumn on valuation vertigo (founder also jailed for insider issues), then a short-squeeze re-acceleration on the November ban, closing the year near ~690,000–770,000 won for the ~+571% full-year print. (Prices approximate; a later 5:1 split rebased quotes.)

**Transferable lesson:** A retail-mania re-rating on a small domestic free float plus heavy short interest is a *reflexive squeeze machine*, not a valuation. Look-through NAV becomes irrelevant while the flow persists, so shorting the "obvious" overvaluation is where capital dies — especially into a regulatory short ban. Risk signature: NAV/DCF ignored, holdco-above-parts inversion, extreme retail concentration, parabolic slope, then violent 40%+ air-pockets that alternate with squeeze spikes. These end not on a catalyst but when marginal retail buying and short-covering fuel exhaust — mean-reversion is eventual but the path is lethal to premature shorts.

### VFS — VinFast Auto (NASDAQ) · $10 → ~$93 peak → ~$6 · ~9x intra-year (round-trip) · ~99% insider-held float
**What the market misunderstood:** The headline "market cap" — briefly north of ~$150–190B, momentarily eclipsing Ford *and* GM combined — was a pure arithmetic artifact, not a market judgment of value. Multiplying ~2.3B shares outstanding by a price set on roughly 1% float does not measure what the enterprise is worth; it measures what a handful of shares changed hands for. With ~99% of stock locked up by founder Pham Nhat Vuong / Vingroup, there was no meaningful supply against which price could be discovered. A stock "worth more than Ford+GM" while selling a fraction of their volume was a tell, not a thesis.

**What actually changed — business vs. re-rating:** Essentially *nothing*. VinFast in December 2023 was the same loss-making, cash-burning early-stage EV maker it was in July — same deliveries, same balance-sheet dependence on Vingroup, same unproven US demand. There was no earnings surprise, no product breakthrough, no order book to justify a >10x move up or the collapse back down. This was a **mechanical float event**, start to finish — a de-SPAC squeeze, not a re-rating grounded in fundamentals.

**Mechanism & timeline:** Completed its Black Spade SPAC merger ~Aug 14–15, 2023 at the $10 SPAC reference. With almost no tradeable supply, thin buying spiked it to an intraday/closing high of ~$93 on Aug 28, 2023. As lockup/float dynamics normalized and insider-sale registrations (reportedly up to ~75.8M shares) surfaced, it fell off a cliff — down ~93% from peak by October, grinding toward ~$6 by December, a full round trip.

**Transferable lesson:** Treat a sub-2% float de-SPAC as a slot machine, not a company. On the long side, the squeeze is real but untradeable in size and unknowable in timing — you are front-running an air pocket. On the short side, it is a *trap*: locate/borrow is scarce, borrow costs are punitive, and mark-to-market pain can bankrupt you before the inevitable reversion arrives. The signal is always structural — insider ownership, days-to-cover, shares actually available — not narrative. File alongside the KB's other float-driven squeezes (VW/Porsche 2008, GME 2021, low-float de-SPACs): **when float is the story, price is fiction.**

### BTBT — Bit Digital (NASDAQ) · ~+627% · ~7x · start mkt-cap ~$70M
**What the market misunderstood:** Entering 2023, BTBT was priced as a sub-scale, cash-burning bitcoin miner left for dead by the 2022 crypto winter — trading near or below the net value of its BTC holdings and cash, with the market ascribing roughly zero value to fleet expansion or any optionality. It understated two things: (1) how violently a tiny, high-beta miner re-rates when BTC itself recovers, and (2) that Bit Digital was positioned to be among the first miners to reframe itself as a compute/infrastructure company rather than a pure commodity play.

**What actually changed — business vs. re-rating:** *Business change* was real but modest: 2023 revenue rose to ~$45M (roughly +39%), the net loss narrowed sharply, the mining fleet scaled toward ~2+ EH/s (management targeting ~2.6 EH/s), and — critically — on ~Oct 23, 2023 the company signed its first GPU-cloud customer (Bit Digital AI, later WhiteFiber), a term sheet for ~2,048 GPUs over three years worth ~$150M gross (~$50M annualized; figures approximate). *Re-rating change* dwarfed the fundamentals: on a ~$70M base, crypto-equity flows plus a nascent AI-compute halo compressed years of hoped-for optionality into a ~7x move — multiple expansion, not earnings.

**Mechanism & timeline:** The dominant driver was beta — BTC recovered ~+155% (roughly $16.5K→~$42K), and small miners trade as leveraged calls on that, so a ~+627% equity move is broadly consistent with amplified BTC exposure on a depressed base. Layered on top: steady 2023 production/hash-rate updates, then the Q4 AI-operations announcement seeding a second narrative. Note the marquee AI-cloud contracts and WhiteFiber branding largely landed in 2024–2025; in 2023 the AI story was mostly a seed, not yet a P&L driver.

**Transferable lesson:** A small miner is effectively a leveraged call option on BTC — in a recovery year the equity can move several times the coin off a beaten-down base, largely independent of execution. The extra kicker: when a commodity miner grafts a credible *second* narrative (here AI/GPU compute) onto that tiny base, the market pays twice — once for the beta, once for optionality it can't yet value — and the two multiply. Screen for depressed-base miners with cheap, real second-narrative optionality ahead of a commodity turn.

### MIGI — Mawson Infrastructure (NASDAQ) · ~5–6x · start mkt-cap ~$18M
**What the market misunderstood:** A genuinely unfollowed nano-cap that owned real US digital-infrastructure — ~120–130MW of PJM-connected data-center capacity running both BTC self-mining and third-party hosting/"digital colocation," plus a legacy energy-markets/curtailment angle — yet was priced as a near-dead equity because a distressed, going-concern balance sheet made it un-ownable for anyone but the smallest crypto-Twitter accounts. The market saw the default risk and missed that the same distress created extreme BTC operating leverage per share on a microscopic base.

**What actually changed — business vs. re-rating:** *Business change:* modest and real. As BTC recovered off its late-2022 lows through 2023, self-mining economics inflected — Q3'23 self-mined BTC revenue rose ~41% QoQ to ~$6.9M (~246 BTC), and a new Energy Markets/curtailment program reportedly added ~$13.7M at ~90%+ gross margin, alongside a ~50MW hosting deal signed around October 2023. *Re-rating change:* dominant. Starting near an ~$18M cap (figures approximate; a 1-for-6 reverse split around Feb 2023 muddies raw price comparisons), even small discovery flows plus BTC beta moved the stock ~5–6x. Very little of the move was durable earnings; most was multiple/attention re-rating off a tiny, distressed base.

**Mechanism & timeline:** BTC troughed late 2022; through 2023 its recovery pulled the whole miner complex, and the smallest, most-levered names moved most. MIGI's thin float and near-zero sponsorship meant marginal buyers set the price. The balance sheet stayed ugly throughout — a large accumulated deficit (~$250M cited), ~$25M debt, notes in default, negative working capital, going-concern language — so the re-rating happened *despite* fundamentals, not because they healed.

**Transferable lesson:** The nano-cap-miner "torque" pattern: the smallest, most-distressed miners deliver the highest BTC beta because operating leverage and a low absolute base amplify every dollar of coin recovery — but that torque is symmetric and comes wrapped in survivorship and dilution risk. Reverse splits, default notices, and going-concern flags mean you can be right on BTC direction and still be crushed by share issuance or a broken balance sheet. Size it as an option, not a position.

### AMAM — Ambrx Biopharma (NASDAQ) · ~+619% · ~7x · start mkt-cap ~$225M
**What the market misunderstood:** Coming out of 2022, Ambrx was a bombed-out, clinical-stage ADC name trading close to cash (shares in the low-single digits, cap likely ~$200–250M), effectively pricing its synthetic-biology conjugation platform at zero optionality. The market treated a differentiated technology — site-specific conjugation using a proprietary non-natural amino acid to attach payloads at fixed positions, giving more homogeneous, stable ADCs than earlier heterogeneous chemistries — as if it were undifferentiated and unfundable. Simultaneously, the entire ADC modality was being re-rated industry-wide in 2023 (catalyzed in part by Pfizer's ~$43B agreed acquisition of Seagen), so the misprice was both company-specific and sector-wide.

**What actually changed — business vs. re-rating:** *Business change:* ARX517, Ambrx's PSMA-targeted ADC, produced genuinely de-risking human data in the APEX-01 Phase 1/2 mCRPC trial — early PSA50 responses (roughly 3/3, 2/3, 2/3 across the ~2.0–2.88 mg/kg cohorts) with a notably clean safety profile (no DLTs, no drug-related SAEs) in heavily pretreated patients, presented at ESMO in October 2023, plus FDA Fast Track in mid-2023; the HER2 asset ARX788 in breast cancer added a second shot on goal. *Re-rating change:* multiple expansion driven by sector-wide ADC enthusiasm and takeout optionality — which converted from theory into a real deal.

**Mechanism & timeline:** Initial APEX-01 PSA data in February 2023 lit the fuse (stock reportedly ~$2.5 pre-catalyst); Fast Track designation mid-year and the fuller ESMO readout in October 2023 sustained the re-rating, compounding into the ~+619% full-year move. The capstone: on January 8, 2024, J&J agreed to acquire Ambrx for $28.00/share cash — ~$2.0B equity value (~$1.8B net of cash), a stated ~105% premium to the January 5 close — closing March 2024. Note the platform's Phase 1 status: efficacy signals were early, small-n, and unselected, so "de-risking" is relative, not definitive.

**Transferable lesson:** The classic clinical-catalyst biotech pattern — a real platform priced near cash plus a single de-risking readout in a hot modality can compound into a multi-bagger, and occasionally straight into an acquisition. The asymmetry lives at the intersection of company-specific data and modality-level sentiment; when both inflect together, and a strategic acquirer needs the modality, the takeout tail is fat.

### PRAX — Praxis Precision Medicines (NASDAQ) · ~5–6x · start mkt-cap ~$375M
**What the market misunderstood:** By late 2022 PRAX was a CNS biotech left for dead. An earlier disappointment in its lead essential-tremor asset PRAX-944 (ulixacaltamide) — a T-type calcium-channel blocker — plus the collapse of its GABA-A depression program had crushed the equity to roughly a "cash-shell" valuation (~$350–400M, likely trading near or below net cash). The market priced the ET franchise for failure and assigned little to nothing for the epilepsy pipeline. What it underrated: the ET program wasn't dead, and the sodium-channel epilepsy assets (relutrigine/PRAX-562/PRAX-628) were maturing into credible, potentially best-in-class candidates.

**What actually changed — business vs. re-rating:** *Business change:* in March 2023 the Essential1 Phase 2 topline read out — the primary endpoint (mADL) missed statistical significance (~p=0.126, hedge on exact figure), but multiple secondary endpoints (TETRAS-ADL, CGI-S, PGI-C) hit nominal significance with clean safety. That was enough to de-risk the mechanism and, after a June 2023 end-of-Phase-2 FDA meeting, to greenlight a Phase 3 (Essential3) by year-end. *Re-rating change:* the stock moved from priced-for-failure back to a credible multi-asset pipeline valuation, with the epilepsy readouts adding optionality on top of the ET restart.

**Mechanism & timeline:** The dominant leg was the March 2023 Essential1 data and the subsequent Phase 3 path confirmation (mid-2023 FDA alignment; Phase 3 initiation by year-end). Alongside, positive early epilepsy signals through 2023 (relutrigine and the broader Na-channel portfolio) compounded the re-rating. Net effect through 2023 was roughly a 5–6x move off a depressed base — one of the year's stronger small-cap biotech recoveries. (Note: PRAX later did a reverse split and saw a far larger Phase 3-driven spike in 2025; don't conflate that with the 2023 move.)

**Transferable lesson:** The "biotech priced for a prior failure, then a positive readout on the same or next asset" comeback. When a name trades near cash after a setback, the market often extrapolates one bad print into terminal failure — so a *mixed-but-directional* rebound readout (primary miss, secondaries hit, program advances) can re-rate the stock several-fold, because you're re-pricing from ~zero, not from fair value. Screen for de-risked-below-cash names with a live next catalyst on the same mechanism.
