# 2025 — Re-Rating Situations

**Thesis of the year:** 2025 was the year the AI-buildout trade broadened out of the megacaps into
*everything that powers, cools, connects, computes-adjacent, or gets treasury-financialized around it* —
plus a violent retail bid on crypto-treasury shells, quantum, nuclear/SMR, rare-earths, and defense.
The purest multibaggers were **corporate-treasury pivots** (a shell buys ETH/SOL and re-rates to NAV
mania), **quantum-computing pre-revenue names**, and **AI-power/grid** plays. The megacaps that doubled
(PLTR, APP) were rare and notable precisely because doubling at $100B+ is hard.

### BMNR — BitMine Immersion (NYSE American)

**Headline:** A ~$4 penny-stock bitcoin miner (starting market cap roughly $25–60M, estimated) that appointed Tom Lee chairman on June 30, 2025, spiked past a ~$161 intraday peak in mid-July (a 10x+ move intra-year, ~40x off its pre-announcement base), then round-tripped to finish 2025 near ~$16–17 — up roughly 4x/+318% on the year yet down ~90% from its own peak.

**What the market misunderstood.** Going in, BMNR was a sub-scale immersion-cooling bitcoin miner nobody covered. The misunderstanding was symmetrical: skeptics dismissed it as a shell, while the eventual buyers treated the pivot as a pure leveraged bet on ETH. Both missed that the real product was not ether but *equity issuance mechanics* — the ability to sell stock at a premium to the crypto it held and convert that premium into more crypto per share. The asset was commoditized; the printing press was the moat.

**Business change vs. re-rating change.** The *business* change was concrete but narrow: BitMine raised a $250M PIPE at $4.50/share (55.5M shares, led by MOZAYYX with Founders Fund, Pantera, Galaxy, Kraken, DCG, and Tom Lee personally), and became a leveraged ETH holding vehicle. It then ran an at-the-market machine that ultimately issued 240M+ shares for $10B+ gross, accumulating ~3.6–3.86M ETH by year-end (part of an $11–13B crypto-and-cash pile) to become the largest corporate ETH holder, targeting ~5% of supply. That is a real balance sheet. The *re-rating*, however, came almost entirely from the multiple: the mNAV flywheel. When shares trade above NAV (mNAV > 1), every ATM share sold *adds* ETH-per-share — accretion — which validates the premium, which supports more issuance. Layer on Tom Lee/Fundstrat sponsorship, retail reflexivity, a "MicroStrategy-for-Ethereum" narrative, and Russell-index flow speculation, and the stock detached from NAV to a large premium at the peak.

**Mechanism & timeline.** June 30: Lee named chairman, $250M PIPE announced, stock +400% intraday. Early-mid July: relentless ETH buying, $1B holdings by July 24, peak ~$161. Q3–Q4: continuous ATM issuance scaled holdings toward multi-billion, but the ever-growing share count and a cooling ETH price compressed the premium — ~$42–44 by early November, ~$16 by year-end. The premium that built the empire evaporated; by mid-2026 mNAV sat *below* 1.0 (~0.84) with shares near $13–16.

**The transferable lesson.** Treasury-strategy re-ratings are reflexivity trades, not asset trades. The signal for the *next* one: a micro-cap with a credible sponsor announces a crypto pivot, prints stock at a widening premium to NAV, and the premium itself becomes the story. Buy the flywheel spinning up; the fatal tell is that the same mechanism reverses. Once mNAV approaches 1, issuance stops being accretive, the sponsor slows buying, dilution overhangs, and a falling underlying turns the premium into a discount — a full round-trip while the "business" (ETH held) keeps growing. The balance sheet compounds; the multiple mean-reverts. Never confuse the two.

### FORD — Forward Industries (NASDAQ)

**Headline:** A 64-year-old medical-case maker (~$10–30M pre-pivot cap, est.) ran from low-single digits to a $31.97 52-week high — a ~10x round-numbered melt-up — on a $1.65B Solana-treasury PIPE, then faded to a discount-to-NAV within weeks.

**What the market misunderstood.** FORD was a genuinely sleepy nano-cap: since 1961 it designed carrying cases for diabetic monitoring kits, barcode scanners, and tablets — a distribution shell with negligible earnings and almost no float attention. Overnight (Sept 8, 2025), it stopped being a case company and became a bet on SOL. The market wasn't repricing a business; it was repricing the *balance sheet* the business was about to hold. Anyone anchored to the accessories P&L missed that the equity had been converted into a leveraged crypto wrapper.

**Business change vs. re-rating change.** Cleanly split: the *business* change was real and enormous — a ~$1.65B PIPE (led by Galaxy Digital, Jump Crypto, and Multicoin, who collectively took >$300M, alongside Bitwise, FalconX, ParaFi, Ribbit, Jupiter, SkyBridge, RockawayX, et al.) that funded 6,822,000 SOL at ~$232 (~$1.58B), >1.25% of circulating supply, making FORD the largest SOL treasury. The *re-rating* change was almost entirely sentiment: premium-to-NAV mania, marquee-sponsor validation (the Galaxy/Jump/Multicoin trio as a stamp of legitimacy), and the Solana-treasury copycat wave. The former is fundamentals; the latter is reflexivity.

**Mechanism & timeline.** Sept 8: PIPE announced, shares +128% pre-market, intraday +75–92%, printing a $31.97 high. Sept 11: PIPE closes. Sept 14–15: SOL deployed. Then the fade — SOL fair value fell from $232 cost to ~$209 (Sept 30, $1.43B) to ~$133 (Nov 30, $920.5M), a >24% unrealized hole. The equity, which briefly carried a fat premium, compressed to ~0.73x mNAV — a *discount* to the very coins it holds (est. per Oak Ridge/sell-side commentary).

**The transferable lesson.** This is the 2025 "digital asset treasury" (DAT) shell-reactivation playbook in miniature: dormant micro-cap + named crypto sponsor + oversized PIPE = instant multibagger. The PIPE anchors the story, the sponsor supplies credibility, and thin pre-deal float supplies the vertical move. But the premium is the trade, and premiums mean-revert: as lockups/registrations approach and the token wobbles, mNAV collapses toward — and through — 1x. The cohort rhymes: BMNR (ETH), SBET/SharpLink (ETH), DFDV (Solana) all spiked on the same template; by late 2025 every SOL DAT traded below 1 mNAV. Most round-trip because you're buying $1 of volatile coin for $2–20 of stock, and only the coin persists.

### DFDV — DeFi Development Corp (NASDAQ, ex-Janover)

**Headline:** A sub-$1 real-estate fintech micro-cap (~$40–50M starting cap, *estimate*) that in April 2025 was seized by ex-Kraken operators, rebranded to a Solana treasury, and finished 2025 up ~853% (company-reported calendar-year figure; peak-to-year-end was a violent round trip) — roughly a 9–10x on the year, more from the pivot low.

**What the market misunderstood.** Janover Inc (JNVR) was a sleepy real-estate lending-software micro-cap trading around/below a dollar (all-time low $0.48, Aug 2024). The market did not price it as an option on a *corporate shell* that could be repurposed. On April 7, 2025 a group of former Kraken executives — led by Joseph Onorati (ex-Kraken CSO, installed as chairman/CEO) — acquired majority control; on April 22 it became DeFi Development Corp (DFDV). Overnight the "business" was irrelevant; the equity became a levered, listed wrapper on SOL.

**Business change vs. re-rating change.** These were distinct. The *business* change: DFDV became a **staked-SOL treasury** whose north-star metric is **SOL-per-share (SPS)** — accreting crypto per share, not just accumulating tokens. It ran its own validators, staking essentially all SOL for native rewards (~8.3% annualized, Q4 2025 *est*). The *re-rating* was narrative and structural: a **premium-to-NAV** "MicroStrategy-for-Solana" story, plus a **yield differentiator** — unlike passive BTC treasuries, staked SOL *compounds*, so the treasury grows in token terms even without buying more.

**Mechanism & timeline.** April: $24M PIPE (Galaxy, Arrington, others) seeds the buy; holdings ~$48M via a discounted BitGo block. Early May: >400K SOL. The stock went parabolic into a **$53.88 peak on May 22, 2025** (a 7-for-1 split muddies per-share comparisons — flag when charting), then broke its 50-DMA the next day and drew down hard. Accumulation continued through the volatility: nearing ~1M SOL by July, and ~**2.22M SOL/SOL-equivalents by Jan 1, 2026**, with SPS growing to ~0.074–0.079 SOL (target: 1 SOL/share by Dec 2028). Financing scaled with the story: a **$112.5M 5.50% convertible note due 2030** (July), a **$5B equity line** (RK Capital), and ~$378M raised across 2025 (*company/est figure*).

**The transferable lesson.** SOL treasuries pitched a genuine edge over BTC treasuries — **staking yield** makes the treasury a compounding asset, so SPS can rise organically. The accretion engine, though, is reflexive: issuing equity *above* NAV buys more SOL per share than it dilutes, so a premium is self-fueling — and *self-destructing* in reverse. When the premium compresses and SOL falls together, the round-trip is brutal (the $54→drawdown path). The durable insight: crypto-per-share accretion is real math but only works while the market grants a premium; the premium is the whole trade, and it is not owned.

### QUBT — Quantum Computing Inc (NASDAQ)

**Headline:** From a sub-$200M nano-cap (~$1–2/share, autumn 2024) through a euphoric decabagger arc into 2025 — a >10x re-rating on essentially zero revenue — that within calendar 2025 itself round-tripped violently ($6.18 low, $25.84 high, ~$10.33 close). *(Caveat/flag: the clean 10x move is the Nov-2024-through-2025 window; strict Jan-open-to-Dec-close 2025 was roughly flat-to-down off an already-elevated ~$12–17 base. Kept in the roster as a mania-cohort exemplar, but its "10x+" is window-dependent — the strict single-calendar-year multiple is weaker than the headline. Starting mkt-cap "well under $500M" describes the pre-surge autumn-2024 launch point.)*

**What the market misunderstood.** QUBT had almost no fundamentals to misprice. 2024 revenue was ~$373K; 2025 revenue reached only ~$682K (Q4 2025 sales ~$198K) — figures more typical of a pre-seed startup than a multi-billion-dollar Nasdaq name. With nothing to anchor valuation, narrative filled the vacuum: "quantum" as the next AI, a photonics angle (thin-film lithium niobate) that sounded differentiated, and a government-contract halo. Price became a function of story and flow, not cash flows.

**Business change vs. re-rating change.** This was almost entirely a re-rating — multiple/narrative expansion plus retail momentum riding sector-wide quantum euphoria — with minimal business change. Real milestones existed but were small: a December 2024 NASA contract (Dirac-3 entropy-optimization for image processing, which spiked the stock ~40% in a day), and the May 12, 2025 grand opening of the Tempe, Arizona TFLN "Fab 1" photonic-chip foundry at ASU Research Park, with a handful of small purchase orders and Fab 2 "planned." None of this generated material revenue; the market paid billions for optionality, not output.

**Mechanism & timeline (in order).** (1) Dec 2024: Google's "Willow" chip breakthrough ignites the whole "Quantum Four" cohort; QUBT's NASA deal and a ~$2.7B government quantum-funding headline supercharge the smallest, most-shortable name. (2) Jan 7, 2025: a $100M placement at $12.25 — then Jensen Huang's "15–30 years away" remark craters quantum stocks; QUBT bleeds toward the ~$6 area by spring. (3) Sustained ~26–27% short interest + heavy retail call-buying make it a gamma/short-squeeze vehicle. (4) Autumn 2025: renewed euphoria and enormous dilution-funded raises — ~$500M (Sept 21) and a $750M placement (Oct 5, 37.2M shares) lifting pro-forma cash to ~$1.55B — drive the ~$25.84 blow-off, which fades into the ~$10.33 year-end.

**The transferable lesson.** Recognize a thematic pre-revenue mania cohort by its tells: a shared buzzword, near-zero revenue, valuation set by story and options flow, and a "smallest/most-speculative" member that outruns the leaders on beta. Float and dilution are the tell within the tell — a thin float squeezes violently upward, but management monetizes the mania through serial ATM/placement issuance (>$1.5B raised here), converting narrative into a fortress balance sheet while transferring blow-off risk to late buyers. The asymmetry is brutal: multi-hundred-percent upside, then round-trips. Cash-rich now, fundamentals still absent — the re-rating, not the business, did the work.

### SBET — SharpLink Gaming (NASDAQ)
**Headline:** A ~$3, sub-$40M micro-cap iGaming affiliate marketer (est. starting mkt-cap ~$25-30M) that announced a Consensys-led ETH treasury pivot in late May 2025, spiked ~+870% (~9x) into June, then round-tripped to close 2025 up only ~+15%.

**What the market misunderstood.** The round-trip *is* the story. SBET entered 2025 as a forgotten sports-betting-marketing shell trading near $3. On the pivot it went vertical — an intraday thin-float squeeze printed as high as ~$79 (a >2,500% eight-day move off $3), with a more durable June high near $40 (52-week high $40.46). Chasers were buying "Lubin's Ethereum company." What the equity was actually *worth* was its ETH stack plus/minus a premium — and by year-end the same shares that had been up 9x were up ~15%. Nothing about the underlying ETH per share justified the peak; buyers paid for narrative and sponsorship, not net asset value.

**Business change vs. re-rating change.** The *business* genuinely changed: a $425M PIPE (69.1M shares at $6.15; ~$6.72 for management) led by Consensys, alongside ParaFi, Electric Capital, Pantera and Galaxy, converted SBET into a leveraged/staked ETH holding vehicle. Proceeds bought 176,271 ETH (~$463M), making it the largest public ETH holder — second only to the Ethereum Foundation — and Ethereum co-founder Joseph Lubin (Consensys CEO) became Chairman. But the *re-rating* was almost entirely premium-to-NAV plus Lubin/Consensys sponsorship. When that premium (mNAV) inverted, the equity collapsed toward — and eventually below — the value of the coins it held (by later in the year SBET traded near 0.83x mNAV, a ~17% NAV *discount*).

**Mechanism & timeline.** (1) Pivot: May 27 announcement, closing ~May 29–June 2, ETH accumulation begins. (2) Spike: euphoria drives the ~9x. (3) The scare: on **June 12** SharpLink filed an S-3 to register resale of ~58.7M PIPE shares; the market misread routine post-PIPE registration as insiders dumping, and SBET cratered ~70-75% after-hours toward ~$8-10. Lubin publicly clarified on X that neither he nor Consensys had sold — "standard post-PIPE procedure." (4) The fade: even after continued ETH buying (holdings later topped $1.3B+, and ~$2.1B was raised over the year), the premium never fully returned, leaving the stock at roughly +15% for 2025.

**The transferable lesson.** The 9x and the +15% describe *the same stock in the same year* — proof that in digital-asset-treasury (DAT) names, timing and premium are everything; the asset can rise while the equity round-trips. The premium is the trade, and it mean-reverts violently. Its cohort twin, **BitMine (BMNR)** — Tom Lee's ETH treasury, launched June 2025 — ran the identical arc: mNAV ~2x in August compressing below 1x. Same asset, same sponsorship playbook, same premium-collapse mechanics. Buy the NAV, never the hype multiple.

### 9992.HK — Pop Mart International (HKEX)

**Headline:** From ~HK$85 at the 2024 close to an all-time high of HK$339.80 (26 Aug 2025) — roughly a 4x move at the peak off a starting market cap near US$14B — before cooling to end 2025 up ~110% for the full year (~HK$180). The "5-6x" scout framing captures the trough-to-peak arc; year-open-to-August-peak was closer to ~4x, and the full-year close was ~2.1x (est.).

**What the market misunderstood.** Entering 2025 Pop Mart was priced as a China-domestic novelty retailer — a "blind box" gacha operator whose economics looked cyclical, hits-driven, and geographically capped. The consensus fear was fad risk inside a single market. What was underpriced was the transition already underway: The Monsters / Labubu was mutating from a mainland collectible into a globally scalable IP franchise with pricing power, resale premiums, and a plush-led product format that travelled across cultures.

**Business change vs. re-rating change.** The *business* change was real and enormous, not a story. H1 2025 revenue hit RMB 13.88B, up 204.5% YoY — surpassing all of FY2024 in six months — with operating profit ~5x higher and net profit up ~396%. Full-year 2025 revenue reached ~RMB 37.1B (+185%) with earnings up ~309%. The engine was overseas: H1 international revenue of RMB 5.59B (+439%), with the Americas up ~1,142% (online +~1,977%) and Labubu/The Monsters contributing RMB 4.81B (+668%), ~35% of group sales. Plush overtook figurines for the first time. Separately, the *re-rating*: investors reclassified Pop Mart from a cyclical toy retailer into a durable global-IP "consumer-franchise" compounder — an explicit Disney/Sanrio comp — pushing peak market cap past legacy toymakers Hasbro and Mattel toward ~US$55-60B (est.). Both the numerator (earnings) and the multiple moved together, which is what produces a multi-bagger.

**Mechanism & timeline.** The catalyst was social virality, not a product spec: TikTok unboxings (1B+ views), and celebrity carry — Blackpink's Lisa (the original seed, from Thailand in 2024), then Rihanna, Dua Lipa, Kim Kardashian, Lady Gaga, BTS's V. Scarcity mechanics (drops selling out, UK sales paused, US$13-16 boxes reselling for hundreds) fed FOMO. Earnings prints ratified the frenzy: the March 2025 FY24 report, the blockbuster August H1 print (shares initially popped on the ~400% profit surge), and continued Q4 momentum. Late-year brought the first cooling — "peak Labubu" debates, ~30% drawdowns around prints, and worry over ~40% single-IP reliance.

**The transferable lesson.** Spot the crossover when a local consumer brand's *overseas* line inflects triple-digits off a small base while a single character achieves unaided global recognition — that is the tell that the comp-set is about to change from toy retailer (low-teens multiple, cyclical) to IP compounder (Disney/Sanrio-style annuity), and the multiple re-rates on top of the earnings surge. The risk is symmetric: franchises earn durable multiples; fads borrow them. IP concentration and resale-froth normalization are the fault lines to watch.

### QBTS — D-Wave Quantum (NYSE)
**Headline:** ~$8 start → ~$46.75 October peak (all-time high, Oct 15 2025) → ~$18–20 year-end (est.); roughly 2.5x on a buy-and-hold but ~8–9x from the January post-crash low (~$5) to the autumn top; entered 2025 at a ~$1.5–2B cap (est., flag).

**What the market misunderstood.** For a decade, D-Wave's quantum *annealing* architecture was dismissed by purists as "not real quantum computing" — special-purpose, not gate-model, unable to demonstrate a clean speedup. The consensus was that gate-model players (IBM, IonQ, Rigetti) owned the future and D-Wave was a stranded science project one financing away from insolvency. What changed was proof: annealing produced a *peer-reviewed, useful* computational-supremacy result before any gate-model rival did.

**Business change vs. re-rating change.** Genuine business milestones were real but small. (1) The March 12–13, 2025 *Science* paper ("Beyond-Classical Computation in Quantum Simulation") claimed the Advantage2 prototype simulated spin-glass magnetic-materials dynamics in minutes versus ~one million years on Frontier — the first supremacy claim on a plausibly *useful* problem, not a contrived sampling stunt (it drew immediate rebuttals, worth flagging). (2) Advantage2 reached general availability May 20, 2025 — a shippable commercial system, the differentiator versus pre-revenue peers. (3) Balance-sheet transformation: a ~$150M ATM in January (avg ~$6.10), then a $400M ATM in Q2 plus ~$99M warrant exercises and ELOC proceeds, lifting cash to a record ~$819M by June 30 (from ~$41M a year earlier) — going-concern risk erased. (4) Revenue grew fast off a tiny base: H1 revenue $18.1M (+289% y/y); FY2025 revenue reportedly up ~179%. Bookings were noisier — FY2025 bookings ~$18.7M, *down* 22% (2024 included an eight-figure system sale) — but post-year-end bookings re-accelerated (>$32.8M through Feb 2026, incl. a $20M Florida Atlantic system and a $10M Fortune-100 QCaaS deal). Honestly, the ~8–9x move was mostly **sector beta**: the "Quantum Four" retail rally, multiple expansion on tiny revenue, and cohort flows. The milestones were the *permission slip*; euphoria did the heavy lifting. Note the arc was violent — Jensen Huang's January "20-year" comment crashed the whole cohort before the *Science* paper reignited it.

**Mechanism & timeline.** Jan: $150M ATM / Huang-driven selloff → Mar 12: *Science* supremacy claim, sharp re-rate → May 20: Advantage2 GA → Q2: $400M raise (dilution into strength) → summer–autumn quantum mania → Oct 15 peak $46.75 → year-end give-back.

**The transferable lesson.** In a momentum cohort, isolate the member with a *falsifiable, real* catalyst. QBTS had a *Science* paper, a shipping commercial system, and paying customers; pure-momentum names like QUBT had story alone — and de-rated harder. Second, a **dilution-into-strength** raise is bullish for a story stock: issuing ~$550M into a euphoric bid converted a going-concern liability into ~$800M+ of runway, structurally de-risking the equity even as it capped per-share upside. Milestone plus fortress balance sheet is what separates a survivor from a lottery ticket.

### MP — MP Materials (NYSE)

**Headline:** ~$20 (early Jan) → ~$50 close (Dec 31), a ~2.5x on the year and a ~5x at the October peak (~$99); starting market cap ~$2.5B → ~$9B. A textbook policy-driven re-rating.

**What the market misunderstood.** Entering 2025, MP was priced as what it superficially was: a single-asset commodity miner. Its economics were hostage to the NdPr (neodymium-praseodymium) price, which China effectively sets by controlling ~90% of refined supply and can flood at will. That framing gave MP a cyclical-miner multiple and a fat downside tail — if Beijing dumped NdPr, MP's margins evaporated. What the market underpriced was the *strategic option value*: Mountain Pass is the only scaled Western rare-earth resource, and rare-earth magnets are a chokepoint for F-35s, guided munitions, EVs, and robotics. In a decoupling world, that scarcity is a national-security asset, not a commodity. The market was valuing the ore body; it was ignoring the geopolitics.

**Business change vs. re-rating change.** These must be split cleanly.
- *Business change (real, cash-flow-level):* The DoD deal (July 10, 2025) established a **10-year $110/kg NdPr price floor** — this is the crux. It structurally removes the China-set commodity-price tail risk that defined the bear case. Add guaranteed offtake (DoD backstops 100% of the new "10X" magnet facility's output, lifting capacity from 3,000 to 10,000 metric tons), $1B in JPMorgan/Goldman construction financing, and downstream *magnet-making* that moves MP up the value chain from digging ore to selling finished components. The **Apple $500M agreement** (July 15) then anchored a blue-chip commercial customer (recycled magnets from Fort Worth, deliveries from 2027).
- *Re-rating change (multiple-level):* MP was reclassified from cyclical miner to strategic national-security asset with a sovereign backstop. DoD taking $400M of convertible preferred (conversion ~$30.03) to become the ~15% largest shareholder is not just capital — it's the US government putting its balance sheet behind the equity. That justifies a fundamentally higher, more infrastructure/utility-like multiple, because the floor truncates the downside distribution.

**Mechanism & timeline.** (1) April 2025 — China tightens export licensing on seven REEs (samarium, dysprosium, terbium, etc.); magnet exports fall ~75%, exposing Western supply-chain fragility. (2) July 10 — DoD public-private partnership: preferred equity + price floor + 10X offtake. (3) July 15 — Apple $500M. (4) Q3-Q4 — momentum and short-covering carry the stock to a ~$99 October peak before consolidating into the $50s. The catalyst chain runs cleanly from adversary action → government backstop → commercial validation.

**The transferable lesson.** A government price floor re-rates a commodity business by *changing the shape of the return distribution, not just its level*. Commodity equities carry a low multiple mostly to compensate for left-tail price risk; guarantee the floor and you delete the tail, so the multiple should mechanically expand even before any earnings change. To spot the next "national-security re-rating," look for the intersection of: (a) a genuine supply chokepoint an adversary controls, (b) an adversary action that makes the dependency politically intolerable, and (c) a domestic player that is the *only* credible substitute — giving it monopoly-of-necessity pricing power. When Washington's cheapest fix is to write a check to that single company, the equity gets a sovereign floor. Hunt this pattern across critical minerals (lithium, gallium, graphite), leading-edge semis (fabs, packaging), and defense/shipbuilding. Timing tell: the re-rating front-runs the cash flows, so the trade is buying the *reclassification*, before the multiple fully reflects the floor.

### CRCL — Circle Internet Group (NYSE)

Underwriters priced a niche, rate-sensitive fintech; the open market immediately repriced CRCL as *the* liquid public proxy for the entire stablecoin thesis. But "regulation as catalyst" IPOs systematically overshoot — the catalyst is a one-time legitimization, while the economics (here, rate-sensitive spread income shared with a distribution partner) are unchanged and mundane. The clarity that sparks the melt-up also invites competition and removes the mystery; multiples that priced optionality revert once the category is merely *legal* rather than *scarce*. Buy the legitimization, respect the overshoot, and underwrite the normalized cash flows — not the peak.

Watch for names whose product has always worked but never mattered; when a scarcity shock (here, grid latency vs. AI buildout speed) reframes the niche as mission-critical, the stock can re-rate on *classification* as much as on cash flow — and the profitability inflection turns a story into a trade.
